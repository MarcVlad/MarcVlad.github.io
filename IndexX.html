About

Let me introduce myself

I am the director of the NGO A.M.OR which teaches digital skills to children facing hardship. Leading an international team, I want to give back to the world, especially to the youth. I advocate for privacy in our society and love sharing ways of protecting one's digital exhaust. I am also promoting OpenSource / OpenAccess content in the projects I am part of as it allows a complete understanding of the digital tool and, reduces the digital gap by sharing resources freely. Since I can recall, I am passionate about maps, and spatial data analysis has been taking more of my time since I committed to Open source Investigation (OSINT) of weapon trafficking in Libya. Also, I always try to grasp the energy sources, socio-political and economic settings, as well as their environmental tenets, wherever I travel to. The new way of producing, consuming and transmitting energy fascinates me, and I believe there are great investment opportunities yet to explore. In the future, I wish to keep solving problems in these sectors, in an opensource framework preferably, and want to work for people in distress - invest myself in environmental relations, sustainable energy systems, and teaching. I believe nonprofits and some international institutions are the enablers of societal improvement and want to keep working for a cause

Work Experience

Executive Director

July 2017 - Present

Association Mondiale pour les Orphelins (A.M.OR)

I have created HiTech4Orphans, an educational program (in graphic design and programming) that will be implemented in 10 countries by 2022 thanks to partnerships with local schools, public institutions and our private/public donors. The beneficiaries receive IT goods and innovative pedagogic tools allowing them to enhance their digital skills. The goal of HiTech4Orphans is to unleash the power of the individual into learning autonomously and being able to integrate the buzzing digital economy.

Teacher

I make them reflect on their motivation, their social interactions, and their future by always asking them which problems they'd like to solve and make a living of it.

Education

Master Degree in Energy Sciences

Sept 2017 - June 2020

University of Geneva / University of Federico II - Naples

My thesis modeled the impact of policies on the attractiveness of photovoltaïc coupled with battery system at the residential scale in Geneva, Switzerland. Through the use of an open-source battery scheduler, I made policy recommendations to boost the profitability of PV-coupled battery systems. I developed strong quantitative skills (R, Excel) and acquired good understanding of renewable integration as well as of the economics behind electricty prices. I also completed a certificate in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) in which I completed various projects (such as spatial data analysis, teledection, risk assessment, database work) and used an interdisciplinary approach to solving sustainability problems by treating large amount of data on humidity indexes, energy consumption, urban fluxes, etc.

Bachelor Degree in Human Geography

Sept 2014 - June 2017

University of Geneva / University of Toronto

My final study project (5.75/6) analyzed, with the lens of political ecology, Canadian gold mining in Guatemala. It gave me a better understanding of the global economy, international relations, human rights, and territorial development. I particularly enjoyed my sociology, history and economics classes during my BA. Social sciences helped me approach the complexity of the world, as I deepdived into history and learned how people process their experience within their communites and according to their values and emotions.
